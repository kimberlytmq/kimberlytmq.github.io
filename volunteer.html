Volunteering at the Zoo

Mandai Wildlife Reserve

June 2019 - Present

Conservation Ambassador

I’ve been volunteering with Mandai Wildlife Reserve since I was 15, serving as a Conservation Ambassador. As an ambassador, I engage guests at the Singapore Zoo, sharing stories about the animals and raising awareness about wildlife conservation. I’ve always loved animals and doing my part for the environment, and it’s been incredibly fulfilling to inspire others to care for wildlife too. These days, I’m usually stationed along the Wild Africa trail, where I talk to guests about African animals such as the cheetahs, giraffes, lions, and zebras. Beyond my regular duties, I also help out with special campaigns and events across the Mandai parks. For example, I participated in World Rhino Day, educating visitors about white rhinos and the importance of rejecting products made from rhino horns.

One of the most exciting things I got to work on as a volunteer was the opening of Bird Paradise. Before the opening, I helped to clean the aviaries before the birds were moved in. We helped to remove leftover construction materials like nails and small plastics to ensure the safety of the birds. It was nice to get a sneak peak of the park before it opened to the public, and it was especially unique to see the aviary without birds and guests. During the big move from Jurong Bird Park, I assisted in cleaning bird carriers and observing the birds as they settled into their new aviaries, monitoring their behaviour and ensuring that newly introduced species were adapting well together.

Zookeeper

During my 2025 summer break, I joined the Keeper Volunteer Programme, where I got to experience life behind the scenes as a zookeeper. I was joined the Marsupials and Small Mammals team, caring for kangaroos, tree kangaroos, otters, and my personal favourite — the rock hyrax.

My days were filled with hands-on husbandry work: cleaning enclosures, feeding animals, preparing enrichment activities, and assisting with welfare checks and training. I even got to help weigh kangaroos, feed meerkats, and watch baby otters grow up! It was such an enriching and eye-opening experience, and I’m so grateful to the Marsupials and Small Mammals team for guiding me throughout.




Paws 4 a Cause

Mercylight • Love the Voiceless

Jan 2025 - May 2025

Volunteering at Mercylight Shelter

As part of the Paws 4 a Cause initiative at Residential College 4, I volunteered with Mercylight shelter, where I helped care for over 50 dogs. On Fridays after classes, we’d head to the shelter to walk the dogs, often doing several rounds so each one could enjoy their time outdoors. After that, we’d help with feeding, cleaning, and tending to wounds or applying medication. For some of the older dogs, we used heat packs to ease their pain and improve mobility. We’d end the evening with a final walk before preparing their bedding for the night. It was tiring at times, especially going straight from classes to the shelter and helping out until nightfall, but it was also incredibly fulfilling. I really loved spending time with the dogs. Each one had such a unique personality, and they were all so sweet in their own ways.

Web Development for Love the Voiceless

Alongside that, I also volunteered with Love the Voiceless as part of their web development team. We built a website using React to showcase dogs available for adoption. It was really meaningful to be able to use my technical skills to contribute to a cause I care about.

I’m always looking for opportunities to learn, grow, and take on new challenges.
